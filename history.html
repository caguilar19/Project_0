The History of the Taco

Although its true origins are unknown, there are theories on where how taco was invented. Jeffrey M. Pilcher, who has studied tortillas for approximately twenty years, theorizes that tacos originally came from Mexican silver miners during the 18th century. Although the tortilla had been a major part of the mesoamerican diet before this, Pilcher claims that it wasn't refered to as a taco until the miners. He argues that the word 'taco' comes from the name for the charges used by the miners. As the dish evolved—especially under industrialization when migrating women brought their cooking skills to Mexico City—the taco came to represent a dish not reserved for the wealthy.

One of the big developments in the history of the taco was the birth of 'el taco al pastor' which began with the arrival of Middle Eastern immigrants—especially Lebanese. The Lebanese immigrants began to sell 'tacos arabes,' consisting of shawarma served in a bread similar to a pita. Eventually, this taco evolved into the famous 'al pastor' as the lamb originally used was replaced by marinaded pork and the tacos were served on tortillas with pineapple. According to Pilcher, this taco became popular during the 1960s as the children of the original Lebanese immigrants began selling the transformed version of the original dish—which can still be found around Mexico.
